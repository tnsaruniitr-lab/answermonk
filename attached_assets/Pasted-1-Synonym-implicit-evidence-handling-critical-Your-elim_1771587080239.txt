1) Synonym & implicit evidence handling (critical)

Your elimination logic currently relies on literal matches:

no quote mentions “UAE” → Missing

Real pages almost never use the exact same wording as the query.

Examples you will constantly hit:

Page text	Meaning	Your current result
“operates across the GCC”	UAE included	Missing
“Middle East fintech”	UAE likely	Missing
“Dubai-based startup”	UAE confirmed	Missing
“available internationally”	geo unclear	Supported (wrong)

So the elimination engine will falsely filter brands — the worst possible error because it produces very strong conclusions.

Fix (small but mandatory)

Create a dimension synonym map + implication rules

Example:

geo:
  UAE:
    direct: ["uae","united arab emirates","dubai","abu dhabi"]
    regional: ["gcc","middle east","mena"]
    implied_support: ["dubai-based","licensed in DIFC"]
    ambiguous: ["global","international"]

Then classify:

direct → supported

regional/implied → weak support

ambiguous → neutral

contradictory → contradicted

You don’t need ML here — just curated expansions + fallback LLM for unclear cases.

Without this, elimination detection will look impressive but be wrong.

2) List ranking bias (LLM behavior quirk)

Listicles heavily bias AI answers even when descriptions are weak.

Example:

Top Corporate Cards UAE
1. Qashio
2. X
3. X
4. Pemo

The model often prefers higher-ordered entities even if descriptions are identical.

Your system currently treats all mentions equally.

Add: positional weight

When extracting brand mentions from list pages:

detect ordered lists / ranking tables

store position index

boost confidence for earlier placements

Otherwise your report will say:

descriptions are similar, unclear why competitor wins

while the real reason is list prominence.

3) Retrieval chunking mismatch (subtle but important)

AI systems don’t read whole pages — they read chunks.

A brand can appear on a page but never in the same chunk as the query terms → effectively invisible.

Your pipeline analyzes at page level.

Add lightweight proximity check

Within extracted text:

Measure distance between:

brand mention

query dimension terms

If far apart → weak evidence
If close → strong evidence

Example:

page mentions UAE in intro and brand in footer → weak retrieval signal

This dramatically improves explanation accuracy without simulating full vector search.

What changes in output quality

Without these:

impressive reports

occasional inexplicable contradictions

With them:

explanations consistently match observed AI behavior

This matters because users will test your tool against real queries repeatedly.

Everything else in your design

Your lifecycle separation (score → analyze) is correct
Your modules are sufficient
Your cost model is realistic
Your reasoning framework is now sound

You don’t need more features.

Final verdict

Yes — this is launch-ready after adding only:

synonym/implication handling in elimination

positional weighting for ranked lists

proximity (same-chunk) relevance check

These are not enhancements — they prevent systematic misdiagnosis.
