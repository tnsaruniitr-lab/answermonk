1. Subject is too long

It is data-rich, but it looks heavy and slightly machine-generated. Open rates usually improve when the subject is shorter and easier to parse.

Your current subject:

has 3 brands

multiple numbers

long visual length

feels like a report, not a hook

Better:

Sandstorm Digital: 12.5% AI visibility vs Digital Gravity 62%

Sandstorm Digital is losing AI recommendations to Digital Gravity

Sandstorm Digital: AI visibility gap vs NEXA / Digital Gravity

2. Too many numbers too early

You are hitting them with:

12.5%

#4.4

62%

#4.7

46%

#2.7

480 queries

16 segments

519 authority sources

That is too dense for cold email. It reduces clarity.

Use fewer numbers:

your appearance rate

one competitor benchmark

maybe one engine-level gap
That is enough.

3. “Rank #4.4” is awkward

It sounds mathematically precise but not naturally meaningful in email. It may also trigger skepticism.

Better:

average rank

Top 3 rate

recommended in X% of prompts

Top 3 rate is usually easier to understand than average rank.

4. “Performance Marketing — 70% (on Claude only)” is confusing

This can backfire because it implies the result is narrow and maybe unreliable.

You should phrase it as:

strongest segment: Performance Marketing on Claude

weakest: ChatGPT and Gemini visibility remains very low

That sounds more diagnostic and less like cherry-picking.

5. “heavily Claude-dependent” is good insight, but sentence is too crowded

This part is actually strong, but needs simplifying.

6. “519 authority sources analyzed” is not very emotionally compelling

It sounds impressive, but it is not the core pain.
For cold outreach, competitor gap matters more than crawl volume.

7. “I can help move you from 12.5% → 30%+”

This is the riskiest line
It sounds like a performance promise without proof. On cold email, that can reduce trust.

Safer:

I think there is realistic room to improve visibility materially, especially on ChatGPT and Gemini

The biggest near-term upside seems to be in lifting weak-engine presence and Top 3 inclusion

8. “Would you be willing to try it out for free?”

Weak CTA.

Why:

sounds needy

lowers perceived value

invites low-intent curiosity

“try it out” is vague

Better:

Want me to send the short teardown?

Worth sharing the 1-page summary?

Open to a short walkthrough?

Better structure

Use this order:

competitive gap

your current position

best/worst insight

why this is not generic

soft CTA
