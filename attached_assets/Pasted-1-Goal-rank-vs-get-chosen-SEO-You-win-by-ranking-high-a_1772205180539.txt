1) Goal: rank vs get chosen

SEO: You win by ranking high and earning the click.

LLM search: You win by being retrieved and then judged “quotable” (trustworthy, directly answers the question, easy to cite). Often the user never clicks.

2) Metrics that matter

SEO: impressions, CTR, positions, backlinks.

LLM search: share of voice in answers, citation frequency, which queries you’re mentioned for, brand/entity recall, and source inclusion in AI features.

3) Content shape: “best page” vs “best passage”

LLMs often pick snippets that are:

directly responsive (1–3 sentence answers),

well-structured (headings, bullets, definitions),

unambiguous and scoped (no fluffy intros).
Q&A / FAQ works because it creates clean extractable passages, but the broader pattern is “passage-level usefulness.”

4) Authority signals shift: backlinks still help, but “entity authority” rises

Backlinks remain useful (they correlate with being visible/credible on the web).

But LLM systems also reward consistent, repeated mentions of your brand/entity across trusted contexts (reviews, comparisons, lists, reputable discussions). This is where Reddit/Quora and other third-party mentions can help more than in classic SEO.

5) “E-E-A-T” becomes operational: show your work

LLM answers are fragile; systems prefer sources that make verification easy:

clear author/editor info,

citations/outbound references to primary sources,

dates/last updated,

methodology, assumptions, definitions.
This increases the chance your page is safe to cite.

6) Freshness matters more for many queries

AI answers get penalized harder when they’re stale or unclear on timing. You need:

visible “updated on” dates where appropriate,

update rhythms for fast-moving topics,

versioning / changelogs for specs, pricing, policy pages.

7) “Two-layer SERP”: sometimes you want to rank on Reddit/Quora, not just your site

In SEO you mainly try to rank your own pages.
In LLM/AI search (and even in Google AI experiences), it can be rational to also:

seed accurate, helpful answers in high-ranking threads,

ensure your brand is mentioned in “comparison / best / alternatives” discussions,

publish canonical pages that those threads can cite.
Because those threads themselves are often what get retrieved/cited.

8) Structured data is less about “rich snippets” and more about disambiguation

Schema still helps SEO, but for AI it additionally helps systems understand what you are (product, org, person, pricing, reviews, FAQ, how-to) and connect entity relationships cleanly.

9) Risk profile changes: hallucination + mis-citation

In SEO, being wrong mostly hurts conversion/brand.
In LLM search, wrong pages can get amplified into answers—especially in health/finance/legal-ish topics—so “trust + clarity” is a gating factor.

10) Distribution strategy expands

Classic SEO: publish + links.
LLM search: publish + be present where models and retrieval systems look:

reputable review sites,

comparisons,

Wikipedia/Wikidata-style entity nodes (where relevant),

Q&A communities,

industry directories,

docs, specifications, and “definition” style pages that are easy to cite.
