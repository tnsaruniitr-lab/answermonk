My view:

Best immediate sequence

#1 noindex thin/broken reports
#10 freshness signals
#7 internal linking
#2 demand-capture pages
#3 vertical/location pages
#8 glossary
#5 trust strip
#4 report depth

Where I differ from your order:

1) Internal linking should come earlier than vertical/glossary expansion.
New pages help only if authority flows into them and out of them properly. Right now, fixing crawl/value distribution is likely higher ROI than just adding more surface area.

2) Demand-capture pages are more important than extra glossary pages.
Glossary is useful for topical authority, but pages like:

AI search visibility software
GEO audit tool
AEO audit
LLM visibility tracking
AI search competitor analysis

are closer to commercial intent and should matter more for actual traffic and conversions.

3) Be stricter on noindex logic.
Not just shareOfVoice === 0 && no competitors. Also noindex pages with:

blank/malformed category or query
near-duplicate intent
too little unique explanatory text
empty engine breakdown
bad slug/data mismatch

You want a broader quality gate, not just one condition.

4) Freshness signals should be machine-readable too.
Not only visible HTML. Add proper structured data / meta where possible. Visible date is good; machine-readable date is better.

5) Trust signals need to be stronger than a strip.
A strip is fine, but not enough by itself. Add:

how many prompts / audits run
which engines
when updated
methodology link
who is behind it
limitations / what score means

6) Report-page depth is high leverage even if partial.
Even without schema changes, adding:

sample prompts
engine-by-engine table
key drivers paragraph
“why this brand appeared” explanation
related reports / competitors

can materially improve usefulness.

7) I agree with skipping URL refactor for now.
Correct decision. Do not break indexed paths unless current URLs are fundamentally unusable. They are not.

What I’d do first in practice

Sprint 1

noindex quality gate
freshness signals
related links on report pages
trust additions on homepage + methodology

Sprint 2

4–6 demand pages
4–6 strongest vertical/location pages

Sprint 3

glossary expansion
report depth enhancements
One caution

Do not create 6–8 use case pages and 6 glossary pages unless each has meaningfully distinct copy and examples. Otherwise you risk scaling thin pages again in a cleaner wrapper.

So overall: good plan, but I’d move internal linking and demand pages up, and I’d make the noindex gate more aggressive.
If the goal is maximum SEO/AEO gain per hour, start with #1, #10, #7, #2.
