Key Insight: Service pages (product descriptions, "About Us") get <2% of citations. AI treats them as promotional, not informational.

Cross-Engine Brand Visibility (ChatGPT AND Gemini)
Brands cited by BOTH engines: 1. Founderfirst (16 Gemini + 7 ChatGPT = 23 total) 2. Magnitt (multiple URLs, ~15 Gemini + ~10 ChatGPT) 3. Wamda (~8 Gemini + ~6 ChatGPT) 4. Wikipedia (Gemini + ChatGPT for entity pages) 5. Enterpriseam (~6 ChatGPT across news articles)

Single-engine skew: - Sadu (33), Menagrid (17), Failory (14) = Gemini-only - JETRO (7), Berkeley Haas (4) = ChatGPT-only

Implication: Cross-training signals require institutional backing (universities, established media) OR multi-format content (PDF reports + comparison articles).

Domain/Regional Patterns
Why Certain Brands Dominate
Unusual Findings
•
LinkedIn beats VC homepages: 6 citations from LinkedIn posts vs. 1-2 for most VC .com sites
•
Foreign government reports cited: JETRO (Japan) gets 7 citations for MENA data—institutional domain authority transcends geography
•
PDF format bias: 9 of top 30 citations are PDFs—file type signals "research"
•
Wikipedia as VC validator: 13 total Wikipedia citations across entities—no VC firm without a page gets cited for brand queries
•
Reddit/Quora cited (1-2 each): Community threads appear for "most active investors" queries—UGC matters
•
Single-article brands: Sadu (33), Vilcap (14), Newsandviews (14) = one URL drives all traffic
•
.me TLD works: Thestartupscene.me gets 12 citations—nontraditional TLDs acceptable if content strong
What Doesn't Work (Anti-Patterns)
•
Brand homepages without context: Most VC firm domain.com/ pages get 0-1 citations
•
"About Us" pages: Zero citations despite 50+ brands having them
•
Press releases on brand domains: 1-2 citations max (Globenewswire beats self-hosted PR)
•
Social profiles (Twitter/X): Zero citations—only LinkedIn works
•
Paywalled content without summaries: Magnitt's premium reports cited via metadata, not full text
Actionable GEO Playbook
Final Insight
Citation success = being the answer, not the asker. Sadu doesn't ask "who are the top VCs?"—it *declares* the answer in a 2026-timestamped URL. Menagrid doesn't pitch investors—it catalogs them in a searchable database. The brands winning GEO are building reference material, not marketing sites.
