What will NOT work as stated (direly needed correction)
❌ “Process ALL accessible pages regardless of category”

If you literally include everything, you’ll pull in a lot of garbage that will:

inflate crawl time

dilute relevance

confuse users (random pages with 0 brands)

generate wrong action items (“get listed on X”) from irrelevant pages

The worst offenders are:

social profiles (LinkedIn/Twitter)

wrapper/redirect URLs (Vertex grounding redirects)

generic directories that don’t mention your category

pages that are not in the same topic space (because LLM citations can be noisy)

Better rule (minimal):
Process all pages except:

redirect wrappers (resolve to final URL first)

pure social/profile pages (skip crawl, just record as “social mention exists”)

inaccessible / non-textual pages

That still gives you ~60–70 pages analyzed, but avoids trash.

The key change you’re missing

You’ve fixed filtering, but you still need surface-type-specific extraction, otherwise you’ll analyze 60–70 pages with the wrong lens and end up with noise.

Required: categorize into surface types and run different extraction

Comparison: multi-brand mentions, position in list, pros/cons, pricing mentions

Eligibility (single-brand product pages): category/geo/audience confirmation, feature specificity, onboarding/pricing signals

Authority (news, Crunchbase/CBInsights): funding, partnerships, licensing, “based in Dubai/UAE”, customer count

Brand-owned / competitor-owned: positioning, keyword clarity, proof, geo claims, structured FAQs

If you don’t do this, your “relevance” will still misfire and you’ll still see many “0 brands found”.
