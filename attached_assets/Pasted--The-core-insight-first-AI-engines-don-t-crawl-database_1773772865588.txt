
The core insight first
AI engines don't crawl databases — they cite readable, structured, authoritative text. Your goal is to make every brand page feel like a Wikipedia article written for a machine.

Best storage architecture: Hybrid
Keep two layers:

Internal DB (Postgres/Supabase) — raw structured data, numbers, rankings, timestamps. For querying and updating.
Public-facing HTML/Markdown pages — auto-generated from the DB, optimised for AI crawling. This is what gets cited.

Never expose raw JSON as your public surface. AI engines ignore it.

Format of each brand page — what to include
Every brand page should have these in plain readable prose, not tables or JSON:

A declarative opening sentence — "Notion is a productivity tool recommended by ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity in 47% of queries about note-taking apps for teams." — This is citation gold. AI engines love pulling exact stat sentences.
Category + use case tags written as full sentences, not just tags
Comparative context — "Notion ranks 2nd behind Linear for project management queries on Claude" — comparative sentences get cited far more than standalone facts
Recency signals — timestamp every data point. "As of March 2026, Notion appears in..." — AI engines heavily weight freshness
Source attribution sentences — list which sources (blogs, Reddit, G2) are causing the AI recommendation. This makes your data uniquely citable.


Schema markup — non-negotiable
Add these to every brand page's HTML:

Organization schema with sameAs, description, url
Dataset schema for your ranking data
FAQPage schema with questions like "Is Notion recommended by ChatGPT?" answered in one clean sentence
BreadcrumbList for category hierarchy

AI engines increasingly use schema to understand relationships between entities.

URL structure matters enormously
/directory/crm/salesforce
/directory/crm/salesforce/chatgpt-visibility
/directory/crm/salesforce/vs-hubspot
The /vs/ comparison pages will get cited the most — that's the query pattern AI engines answer constantly.

The single biggest lever: FAQ blocks
Write 5–8 questions per brand page in natural language that match real AI search queries:

"Is Salesforce recommended by AI assistants?"
"What CRM does ChatGPT recommend for enterprise?"
"How visible is Salesforce on Perplexity?"

Answer each in 1–2 sentences with a specific data point. These get pulled verbatim into AI answers.

What to avoid

JavaScript-rendered content (Vercel/Next SPAs without SSR) — AI crawlers often don't execute JS
Paginated tables with no text context
Storing everything behind login/auth walls
Duplicate thin pages (one per engine without unique insight per page)


Summary priority order

SSR/static HTML pages with prose-first layout
FAQ schema blocks with stat-rich answers
Comparison pages (/brand-a-vs-brand-b)
Freshness timestamps on every data claim
Dataset + Organization schema markup
Clean semantic URL hierarchy
