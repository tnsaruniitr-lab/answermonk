1) Missing: Query decomposition (the most important gap)

Right now the system treats the query as one thing:

“best corporate cards for startups UAE”

But LLM answers are assembled from sub-questions, not a single retrieval step.

Internally the model resolves something like:

what products qualify as corporate cards?

which operate in UAE?

which target startups/SMEs?

which have credibility?

which have differentiating features?

Different sources satisfy different sub-questions.

So a competitor may rank higher even if:

they are mentioned less overall

they have weaker descriptions

they appear on fewer Tier-1 sites

because they dominate a critical sub-question.

Your current analysis would mis-diagnose this.

Add: Sub-intent attribution layer (small implementation, huge accuracy gain)

Before scoring brands, split the query into intent dimensions.

Example:

{
  "category": "corporate cards",
  "geo": "UAE",
  "audience": "startups",
  "evaluation": "best"
}

Then map each citation to which dimension it supports.

Now your reasoning changes from:

Brand X appears more often

to

Brand X is consistently cited for the geo + audience constraints, which are decisive filters, so it survives narrowing while others drop out

This dramatically improves correctness and makes reports feel “intelligent” instead of statistical.

Implementation cost: one lightweight LLM call per query.

2) Missing: Negative evidence handling

Currently you analyze presence.
LLMs are heavily influenced by absence and contradiction.

Examples that break your system:

A source says competitor supports UAE → yours doesn’t mention location

A review lists competitor pricing → yours missing pricing entirely

A comparison excludes your brand category

The model eliminates candidates during reasoning.
You need to detect why a brand gets filtered out, not just why another gets boosted.

Add: Candidate elimination detection

For each brand per sub-intent dimension:

Detect:

Case	Meaning
Explicit support	boosts confidence
Neutral / missing	weakens confidence
Explicit contradiction	eliminates candidate

Then your report can say:

You are not mentioned as operating in UAE in any cited source. The model therefore filters you out during geographic narrowing.

This is often the real reason rankings differ.

Implementation: simple rule layer after quote extraction.
